Quick hummus recipe

This recipe makes a quick and tasty, no mess hummus. It has been adapted from several different recipes I've read over the years.

Hummus is a delicious thick paste that is widely used in Greek and Middle Eastern dishes. It is very tasty with salad, grilled meat and pitta bread..

Ingredients

  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas (garbanzo beans)
  • 175g tahini
  • 6 sun-dried tomatoes
  • Half a red bell pepper
  • A pinch of cayenne pepper
  • 1 clove garlic
  • A pinch of olive oil

Instructions

  1. Peel the garlic and chop it coarsely.
  2. Remove the seeds and stem from the bell pepper, and cut it into thick pieces.
  3. Put all foods in a food processor.
  4. Process all ingredients to a paste.
    • If you want a "thick" hummus, process it for a short time.
    • Chop longer if a "smooth" hummus is desired.

For a different flavor, you can try mixing a small measure of lemon and cilantro, chili, lime and chipotle, harissa and mint, or spinach and feta cheese. Taste it and confirm that it is just right.

Storage

Refrigerate the finished hummus in a sealed container. You should be able to use it for about a week after making it. If it starts to bubble, you should definitely discard it.

Hummus is suitable for freezing; you should thaw it and use it within a couple of months.
